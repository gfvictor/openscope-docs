# Offices

### What is it?

The central registry of the agency's operational units, encompassing the headquarters and all its active branches.

### What is it for?

To map the company's physical structure, distributing responsibilities and defining who manages which region, as well as organizing which clients and employees belong to each office.

### How is it used?

The dashboard displays a monitoring list containing the Office name (e.g., Toyohashi Headquarters, Nagoya Branch), the Prefecture, the responsible Manager, their position, and the unit's phone number.

Clicking on an office from the list opens a management panel divided into three tabs:

1. **Office Information:**
   - Complete address;
   - Phone and fax;
   - Direct supervisor's data and their position;
   - Branch's registration date in the system.

2. **Clients:**
   - List of client companies linked to this branch and the total number of active allocations;
   - Nominal list of employees allocated to the selected client.

3. **Managers:**
   - List of all administrators and system operators linked to the office, with restricted or full data access.

### Expected Result

Decentralization with absolute control. The agency can scale its operations by opening new branches, keeping each core isolated and organized, without losing the capacity for centralized auditing and management.
